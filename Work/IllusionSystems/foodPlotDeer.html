Frequently Asked Questions: Deer Food Plots

Want to learn more about deer food plots? Check out our FAQ for quick answers and expert tips.

What’s the best location for a deer food plot?

A secluded, low-pressure spot near bedding areas or travel corridors is prime. Look for naturally fertile soil, good moisture retention, and a spot with enough sunlight (at least 4-6 hours daily) to support healthy growth.

How do I keep deer from wiping out my food plot too early?

Heavy browsing can kill a young plot before it gets established. If deer pressure is high, consider temporarily fencing off new plots, using a larger plot size, or mixing in fast-growing plants that can handle the pressure.

Do I need to fertilize my deer food plot?

Yes—good soil grows better plots. A soil test will tell you exactly what’s needed, but in general, most deer food plots benefit from lime (to balance pH), nitrogen (for growth), phosphorus (for root strength), and potassium (for plant health).
