Nannycare Formula FAQs

Is Nannycare goat milk formula suitable for babies with lactose intolerance?

Nannycare goat milk formula does contain lactose, which is the naturally occurring sugar found in goat's milk. However, some babies with lactose intolerance may still be able to tolerate goat milk formula as it has lower levels of lactose compared to cow's milk.

It’s always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional to determine the best feeding option for babies with lactose intolerance.

Are there different stages of Nannycare formula available?

Yes, Nannycare offers different stages of goat milk formula to meet the changing nutritional needs of growing infants. These stages typically include Stage 1 Nannycare (From Birth), Stage 2 Nannycare (6+ Months), and Stage 3 Nannycare (12+ Months).

Each stage is carefully formulated to provide the necessary nutrients for the specific age group. It’s recommended to follow the guidelines provided by healthcare professionals when transitioning to a different stage of formula.

Is Nannycare goat milk formula organic?

Nannycare goat milk formula is not certified organic, but it is made with high-quality goat milk sourced from goats that are free to graze and fed a primarily natural diet. The formula also avoids the use of artificial colors, flavors, and preservatives, and strives to provide simple, wholesome nutrition for babies.
