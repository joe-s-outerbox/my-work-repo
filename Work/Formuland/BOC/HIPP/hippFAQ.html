The Story Behind HiPP Formula Brand

The HiPP baby formula brand was founded in Pfaffenhofen in the late 19th century by Joseph Hipp, whose wife had trouble breastfeeding her twins. As a baker, he solved his twins' nutrition problems by baking rusks and enriching their milk with rusk flour.

In the mid-20th century, Georg Hipp started manufacturing weaning food and switched to packaging in jars.

The HiPP product range expanded to include organic farming, and Claus Hipp travelled to raise awareness and build a network of organic farms. The family-owned Ehrensberg farm near Pfaffenhofen converted to organic production, and HiPP became a pioneer in organic farming.

For more than 50 years, the HiPP company has been associated with thoughtful and conscientious treatment of the environment, people, and the economy. Every HiPP organic baby formula product comes with a personal assurance from Claus Hipp that it is of the highest caliber.

HiPP Formula FAQs

Are you new to the world of HiPP baby formula and curious about its advantages? Let's address some commonly asked questions to help you make an informed decision:

Do you have to boil water for HiPP formula?

Enhance the purity of your baby's meals by starting with boiled water before mixing it with HiPP formula. This simple step ensures the safety of your little one while preserving the essential minerals in the water, creating a nourishing blend for your baby's needs.

How do you prepare HiPP formula?

To prepare HiPP formula, follow these guidelines:

  1. Use the scoop provided in the pack. Do not use any other scoop or measuring device.
  2. Do not alter the strength of the formula by adding extra water or scoops. Follow the recommended quantity of water and measuring spoon specified for each stage.
  3. For hygiene reasons, always prepare fresh feeds and avoid preparing them in advance. Use the prepared formula within 2 hours. Discard any leftover formula after feeding.
  4. Do not heat the formula in a microwave, as it may create hot spots and cause scalding. Use alternative methods for warming the formula, such as a bottle warmer or warm water bath.
  5. Introduce a cup or beaker as soon as possible to prevent prolonged or frequent contact of teeth with milk feeds, which can increase the risk of tooth decay.
  6. Use the sachet of formula within 3 weeks of opening to ensure freshness and quality.

By following these instructions, you can ensure the proper preparation and safety of HiPP formula for your baby's consumption.
