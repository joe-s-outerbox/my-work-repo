Bariatric Surgery Options

Our surgical weight loss solutions include:

Gastric Sleeve (VSG) Surgery

During this weight loss surgery, we remove a portion of the stomach to create a smaller, sleeve-shaped organ that will hold less food. This reduction in stomach size helps decrease hunger and food intake, aiding weight loss. Unlike other bariatric surgeries, this procedure does not reroute the intestines, making it a simpler option for those looking to lose weight surgically.

Lap Band Surgery

Also known as adjustable gastric banding, Lap Band surgery is a minimally invasive procedure that surrounds the upper stomach with a silicone band to create a smaller stomach pouch. The reduced capacity leads to earlier satiety with less food, promoting sustainable weight loss.

Gastric Bypass

Gastric bypass surgery, also called Roux-en-Y, is a two-step weight loss operation that decreases stomach size and modifies the digestive tract to restrict food intake and nutrient absorption.

SADI Surgery

SADI surgery is a streamlined version of the duodenal switch that creates a single connection between the stomach and the small intestine, shortening the nutrient-absorbing intestine to reduce calorie intake and enhance weight loss. This method promotes significant, sustainable weight loss while lowering the risk of complications common in more complex bariatric surgeries.

Gastric Band Revision

Once the most popular weight loss surgery worldwide, gastric bands can often be revised into a gastric bypass or sleeve or SADI procedure.

Gastric Sleeve Revision

If you're seeking improved outcomes or experiencing issues after gastric sleeve surgery, a VSG revision can adjust your initial procedure. Known for its simplicity and efficiency, gastric sleeve revision has a low complication rate and delivers significant results.

The ideal candidate for each weight loss surgery varies. Our experienced team can guide you through the decision-making process and answer questions along the way.
