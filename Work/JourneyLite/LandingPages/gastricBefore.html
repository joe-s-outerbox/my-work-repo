What Is Gastric Sleeve Surgery?

Also called a sleeve gastrectomy, gastric sleeve surgery is a minimally invasive procedure during which the surgeon creates a smaller, sleeve-shaped stomach by removing a portion of the stomach. The surgery significantly decreases the amount of food the stomach can hold, helping patients feel full sooner and consume fewer calories. It is performed laparoscopically, involving small incisions and a camera for guidance, resulting in a quicker recovery time and less postoperative discomfort.

Gastric Sleeve Timeline: What to Expect

No two patients are the same, and individual results can vary widely. However, after performing thousands of gastric sleeve surgeries, JourneyLite surgeons have identified common patterns in patient progress at different intervals. Here’s a general guideline:

3 Months After Surgery

At three months post-surgery, patients at JourneyLite often see significant progress in their weight loss journey. Here's what you can expect:

  • Weight Loss: Many patients lose about 30% of their excess weight.
  • Diet: You will transition from pureed and soft foods to more solid foods. Portion sizes will still be small.
  • Activity Level: Moderate physical activities such as walking or swimming are recommended. Increased energy levels allow for more participation in daily activities.
  • Health Improvements: Many patients report reduced symptoms of conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and sleep apnea.

6 Months After Surgery

At six months, the changes become more noticeable, and the weight loss journey continues to be rewarding.

  • Weight Loss: Patients often lose 50-60% of their excess weight.
  • Diet: Your diet will include a variety of healthy solid foods. It is important to follow nutritional guidelines to avoid deficiencies.
  • Activity Level: You can gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Strength training can be incorporated to build muscle mass.
  • Health Improvements: Continued improvement in chronic conditions. Increased mobility and decreased joint pain are common.

9 Months After Surgery

At nine months, significant physical and emotional changes are evident.

  • Weight Loss: Patients often lose 50-60% of their excess weight.
  • Diet: Your diet will include a variety of healthy solid foods. It is important to follow nutritional guidelines to avoid deficiencies.
  • Activity Level: You can gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Strength training can be incorporated to build muscle mass.
  • Health Improvements: Continued improvement in chronic conditions. Increased mobility and decreased joint pain are common.

One Year After Surgery

One year post-surgery marks a major milestone with substantial weight loss and lifestyle changes.

  • Weight Loss: Many patients reach their goal weight, having lost 70-80% of their excess weight.
  • Diet: Your diet should be well-established with a focus on long-term healthy eating habits. Regular follow-ups with a nutritionist can help.
  • Activity Level: High levels of physical activity are encouraged. Many patients participate in sports or fitness programs.
  • Health Improvements: Significant improvements or resolution of obesity-related conditions. Enhanced quality of life and psychological well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions About Gastric Sleeve Surgery

Even after reading our patients' gastric sleeve before-and-after stories, you likely have some questions. JourneyLite is here to provide the information you need.

AWho Is a Good Gastric Sleeve Surgery Candidate?

Good candidates typically have a body mass index (BMI) of 35 or higher. Those with a BMI of at least 30 who have health conditions such as diabetes or hypertension also may qualify. We'll perform a thorough medical evaluation to determine eligibility.

What Are the Risks Associated With a Gastric Sleeve?

As with any surgery, there are risks involved. Potential complications include infection, bleeding, and adverse reactions to anesthesia. Nutrient deficiencies and gastrointestinal issues are possible long-term risks. Our team will discuss these in detail during your consultation.

Will I Need to Follow a Special Diet After Gastric Sleeve Surgery?

Yes, following a specific diet is crucial for recovery and long-term success. Initially, you'll start with a liquid diet, gradually progressing to pureed and then solid foods. Your healthcare team will provide detailed dietary guidelines to ensure you get the necessary nutrients while promoting weight loss.
