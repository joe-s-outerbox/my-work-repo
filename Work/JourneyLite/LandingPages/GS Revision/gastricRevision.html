Gastric Band Revision Options

Most of the time, we can perform a gastric band revision in a single operation. However, there are times when we must stage the surgery. This means we must remove the band first and then return a few months later to complete the procedure. We only do this if we find excessive scarring during the operation. JourneyLite Physicians has extensive experience in lap band revision surgery.

These operations take a little longer than primary procedures. Typical operative times are:

  • Band to Sleeve Conversion: one to two hours, outpatient or overnight stay
  • Band to Bypass Conversion: two to three hours, overnight stay
  • Band to SIPS/SADI Conversion: two to three hours, overnight stay

Return to work for all procedures is one to two weeks for a desk job and around four weeks for a strenuous or lifting job.

Why Opt for a Gastric Band Revision?

Every patient responds to surgery differently, and every surgery is different. There are two main reasons a revision may be appropriate after a gastric band procedure. The first is a postoperative complication. Another is inadequate weight loss after the initial procedure.

Complications include band erosion and band slipping, among others. The most common solution is to surgically remove the band system through a revisional surgery.

Not everyone achieves lasting weight loss success with a gastric band. Initially, when a patient experiences weight regain, we may investigate to identify if they can modify any behaviors to restart weight loss. If behavior modifications fail to achieve the desired result, we explore gastric band revision options.

Gastric Band Revision Advantages

Gastric band revision surgery can provide significant benefits for patients who haven't met their desired weight loss goals or who experience complications. Here are some key advantages of opting for a gastric band revision:

  • Improved Weight Loss: Patients who didn't lose enough weight initially may see better results after a revision.
  • Resolution of Complications: Issues like band slippage or erosion can be corrected, improving overall health and comfort.
  • Enhanced Quality of Life: By addressing weight loss and complications, patients often experience a better quality of life post-revision.
  • Personalized Treatment: The revision can be tailored to your needs and challenges, increasing the likelihood of success.
  • Minimally Invasive Options: Many revisions can be performed using minimally invasive techniques, reducing recovery time and risks associated with surgery.

Gastric Band Revision Details

Gastric band revision surgery involves a series of meticulous steps to ensure the best outcomes for patients. The JourneyLite team, with years of expertise, approaches each component of the procedure with exceptional care and precision. The first step is carefully removing adhesions and exposing the gastric band buckle. Following this, the team works to restore the natural gastric anatomy by removing the sutures that anchored the band in place.

Once the sutures are removed, the next step is to cut and remove the gastric band. This process requires great skill to avoid damaging the surrounding tissues. After the band is successfully removed, the stomach's natural anatomy is restored. The JourneyLite team then moves forward with the appropriate revision to a gastric sleeve, gastric bypass, or the SIPS/SADI procedure.

Frequently Asked Questions About Gastric Band Revision

Gastric band revision is a serious procedure, and patients naturally have questions about it. Here are some of the most common queries.

What Is Gastric Band Revision Surgery?

Gastric band revision surgery is a follow-up procedure performed to address complications or insufficient weight loss from an initial gastric band surgery. It can involve conversion to a gastric sleeve, gastric bypass, or the SIPS/SADI procedure.

Who Is a Candidate for a Gastric Band Revision?

Candidates for a gastric band revision include patients who experience complications such as band slippage or erosion or have not achieved their desired weight loss despite following recommended dietary and lifestyle changes.

How Effective Is Gastric Band Revision Surgery for Weight Loss?

A gastric band revision can be highly effective for weight loss, especially for patients who did not achieve sufficient results with the initial procedure. The success of the revision largely depends on the patient's adherence to dietary and lifestyle recommendations post-surgery.

How Long Is the Recovery Period After Gastric Band Revision Surgery?

Recovery times can differ depending on the patient's overall health and the specific procedure. Generally, patients with desk jobs can return to work within one to two weeks, while those with strenuous or lifting jobs can return within four weeks.
