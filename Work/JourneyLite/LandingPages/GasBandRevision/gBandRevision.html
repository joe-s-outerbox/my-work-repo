What Is Gastric Sleeve Revision?

A gastric sleeve revision is a procedure to modify a previous sleeve gastrectomy. It typically involves converting it into another type of bariatric surgery, such as SIPSor gastric bypass. VSG revision is often aimed at patients who have experienced changes in their gastric sleeve size, requiring an adjustment to the original surgery for continued weight loss success.

Am I a Candidate for Gastric Sleeve Revision?

Ideal candidates for VSG revision are patients who initially experienced significant weight loss with a sleeve gastrectomy but have since regained weight or those who have not succeeded in reaching their goals. This is often due to changes in the size of the gastric pouch. A thorough evaluation by a healthcare provider, including reviewing your weight loss history and possibly conducting gastrointestinal X-rays, is essential to confirm if a gastric sleeve revision is the right step. Ultimately, this procedure is aimed at individuals seeking to reinvigorate their weight loss journey after the initial gastric sleeve surgery. Additionally, gastric sleeve revision can also be considered for those who have experienced complications or issues from their initial surgery, aiming to rectify problems and improve overall outcomes.

Reasons Gastric Sleeve Revision Is Needed

VSG revision may be necessary due to the following:

  • Gastric Sleeve Dilation or Stretching: Natural adaptation or overeating can stretch the sleeve , diminishing the surgery's effectiveness.
  • Inappropriate Weight Loss: Both insufficient and excessive weight loss can make gastric sleeve revision necessary.
  • Diet Non-Adherence: Failing to follow the strict post-surgery diet can lead to overeating and pouch stretching.
  • Reflux and Heartburn: Increased acid reflux or heartburn post-sleeve gastrectomy can necessitate VSG revision.
  • Complications from Initial Surgery: Issues like leaks or strictures from the first surgery might require corrective gastric sleeve revision.

Gastric Sleeve Revision Cost

Typically, the cost for a gastric sleeve revision ranges from $15,000 to $18,000. This range is indicative and can vary based on factors including:

  • Hospital Fees: These can vary significantly based on the facility. You can opt for a surgery center to bypass high hospital fees.
  • Type of Revision Surgery: Different revision techniques can have varying costs.
  • Location: Surgery costs differ based on the city you’re located in.
  • Additional Costs: Pre-operative tests, post-surgery care, and follow-up appointments may add to the total expense.

Benefits of VSG Revision

A VSG revision offers many benefits for those seeking to revitalize their weight loss journey:

Enhanced Weight Loss

Gastric sleeve revision surgery is tailored to reignite the weight loss process for individuals who have seen diminishing results or weight regain after their initial sleeve gastrectomy.

Rectification of Prior Complications

Should complications from the primary surgery arise, a gastric sleeve revision serves as a corrective measure, often leading to improved gastric function and overall well-being.

Increased Satiety

Adjustments made during the VSG revision surgery are designed to help patients achieve a lasting feeling of fullness, aiding in the control of portion sizes and total caloric intake.

Renewed Commitment to Health

Undergoing a gastric sleeve revision can be a catalyst for patients to renew their dedication to a healthy lifestyle, ensuring better adherence to nutritional guidelines and physical activity.

Frequently Asked Questions About Gastric Sleeve Revision

Exploring the possibility of a VSG revision often brings up important questions. Here are some of the most commonly asked questions to help you understand this procedure better. For additional information, contact our officefor assistance.

Can You Have Gastric Sleeve Surgery Twice?

In most cases, gastric sleeve surgery is a one-time procedure. However, if issues arise or if weight loss goals are not met, a VSG revision surgery, which may involve different techniques or converting to another type of bariatric surgery, can be considered.

What Are the Risks Associated with Gastric Sleeve Revision?

Like any surgery, gastric sleeve revision carries risks such as infection, bleeding, and anesthesia-related complications. Gastric sleeve revision is a generally safe procedure with a low risk of complications. There's a minimal chance of experiencing stomach leaks or nutritional deficiencies. Always consult with your surgeon to discuss potential complications prior to surgery.

How Long is the Recovery Time for a VSG Revision?

The recovery from a gastric sleeve revision typically mirrors that of the initial sleeve surgery. Most patients can resume normal daily activities within a few weeks. However, complete recovery, including total adjustment to dietary changes and healing, can extend over several months. Recovery times for VSG revision vary, but it's important for patients to follow post-operative care guidelines closely to ensure a smooth and effective recovery process.

Is Gastric Sleeve Revision Covered by Insurance?

Coverage depends on your insurance plan. Some insurers provide coverage for the gastric sleeve revision cost if it's medically necessary. It's essential to check with your insurance provider for specific coverage details. We are in-network with most insurance companies, including Blue Cross/Blue Shield, Anthem, Humana, Aetna, Cigna, Medical Mutual and others. We also offer financing optionsto fit all budgets.

How Does Gastric Sleeve Revision Differ from the Original Surgery?

Gastric sleeve revision may involve different surgical techniques, such as resizing the stomach pouch or converting to a different bariatric procedure. The approach depends on individual circumstances and the reasons for revision.

How Common Is a Gastric Sleeve Revision?

The likelihood of needing a VSG revision varies, influenced by factors such as the expertise of the original surgeon and the patient's specific needs and goals. The estimated rates of VSG revision surgeriesare 4.7% at five years, 7.5% at seven years, and 12.2% at ten years post-procedure.
