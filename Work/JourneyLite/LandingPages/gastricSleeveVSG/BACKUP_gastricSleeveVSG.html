There are a couple of different names to describe the gastric sleeve procedure:

  • vertical gastrectomy
  • sleeve gastrectomy
  • vertical sleeve gastrectomy
  • VSG

These are all describing the same type of surgery, which is overall a newer procedure being performed for weight loss. It is done with the laparoscopic technique, similar to the Lap Band and Lap Gastric Bypass. It involves removing the portion of the stomach that functions mainly as a storage tank for food to make it much smaller, so it doesn't take much to make you feel full. This is similar in concept to the Lap Band System, although most patients report a lot less hunger after a sleeve than a band. Most studies show that the weight loss with a sleeve is slightly less than with a gastric bypass but slightly more than with a Lap Band. There are some advantages and disadvantages to this procedure:

Advantages of the vertical sleeve gastrectomy (VSG):

  • Since there is no mechanical device, there are fewer food intolerances with vertical sleeve than with the Lap Band.
  • Weight loss is much faster with sleeve than with the Lap Band.
  • Removal of the portion of stomach that secretes hunger hormones such as ghrelin can result in better hunger reduction than with the Lap Band.
  • There is no implantable device, so things like band slips and erosions aren't a concern down the road with the vertical sleeve gastrectomy.
  • The risk is lower than with gastric bypass, yet the weight loss is similar.
  • Since there is no device to adjust, the follow-up regimen not as intense as for the Lap Band.
  • If weight regain occurs it can easily be converted to a gastric bypass or SIPS which will likely lead to additional weight loss.

Disadvantages of the vertical sleeve gastrectomy (VSG):

  • Since this is a newer procedure (15+ years) it is less time-tested than gastric bypass.
  • Unlike the Lap Band, it is non-adjustable.
  • Unlike the Lap Band, it is non-reversible.
  • The short-term risk is slightly higher than with Lap Band, however the long-term risk is much lower as there is no foreign body.
