The Superiority of Dry Carbon Fiber

Dry carbon fiber is produced by manufacturing "pre-preg" carbon fiber material. Here's a breakdown of why this process results in the highest-quality carbon fiber parts:

  • Pre-Preg Material: This term refers to carbon fiber that has been pre-impregnated with resin. The precise amount of resin ensures optimal strength and weight characteristics.
  • Molding and Vacuum Sealing: The pre-preg carbon fiber is placed into a mold to achieve the desired shape. It is then vacuum-sealed to cure. This step, known as the autoclave process, ensures that the part is formed without air pockets or pinholes.
  • Autoclave Process: This high-pressure, high-temperature curing process solidifies the carbon fiber, creating an incredibly strong yet lightweight part. This method contrasts with the "wet" production method, which often incorporates ABS plastic or fiberglass.
  • Highest Quality: The autoclave vacuuming process produces the purest form of carbon fiber parts. The absence of air pockets and minimal pinholes ensures that the final product is aesthetically flawless and superior in performance.
