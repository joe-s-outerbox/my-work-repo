Why Become an Ohio Attorney Notary Public?

As an Ohio attorney notary public, you'll be qualified to notarize documents on behalf of your clients without needing a third party, ensuring accuracy and efficiency. This added capability differentiates your practice, builds client trust, and increases your service offerings.

Steps to Becoming an Attorney Notary in Ohio

The following Ohio attorney notary rules must be observed if you want to take on the added responsibilities of a notary public in this state.

Start your notary career today with our online program:

1. Eligibility Requirements

You must be a licensed attorney in good standing with the Ohio Supreme Court.

2. Mandatory Training

Ohio Notary Services provides comprehensive online training for attorneys. This training covers all necessary aspects of Ohio notary law, procedures, and best practices.

  • Course Content: The training includes detailed modules on the responsibilities of a notary, proper notarization procedures, and Ohio-specific notary laws.
  • Duration: The training is self-paced and can be completed in approximately three hours, but you can take longer if needed.
  • Accessibility: The course is available online 24/7, allowing you to complete it at your convenience.
  • Study Guide: When you sign up for the class, you'll receive a study guide to help you throughout the course.

3. Application Process

Once you've completed your training, you'll need to do the following:

  • Submit your application to the Ohio Secretary of State. You'll be asked for your attorney registration number, business or residential address, and proof of course completion. You'll be required to pay a $15 application fee when you submit your application.
  • Once approved, the Ohio Secretary of State will issue your commission to you. Assuming you remain in good standing, our commission will be good for a lifetime. Your information will be posted on the Secretary of State's website.
  • Purchase necessary notary supplies, such as stamps and journals, from the Ohio Notary Store. Use code "FREE" for free shipping on supply bundles.

Ohio Attorney Notary Application FAQs

Below, we've answered a few common questions about the Ohio Attorney Notary Application process. Please contact us if you'd like additional information.

How Long Does it Take to Complete the Training?

The training can be completed in as little as three hours, though you can take longer if needed, as the course is self-paced.

Am I Required to Take an Exam?

Attorneys are not required to take an exam to become a traditional Notary Public in Ohio. Provided you remain in good standing with the Ohio Supreme Court, your commission will remain in effect for your lifetime.

How Much Does it Cost Attorneys to Take the Notary Public Course?

As of this writing, the course fee for the New Applicant Class for Attorneys is $75

Can an Attorney Become a Remote Online Notary (RON)?

You must first become a traditional Ohio Notary Public before you can take a course to become a RON. Once you've received your authorization, you'll be able to perform notarizations online. Keep in mind, you must renew your RON commission every five years.
