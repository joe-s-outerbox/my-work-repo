What are the Typical Specs For Dry Vans?

Due to its ubiquity, dry van trailers have become standardized, with minimal variations between the models. The maximum freight weight for dry vans is between 42,000 and 45,000 pounds. There are two standard sizes for the main deck of a dry van trailer.

The smaller dry van size is 48’-0” in length and either 8’-0” or 8’-6” wide. The total internal space within these vehicles is typically 3,555 cubic feet. Larger dry van trailers are 53’-0” long and 8’-6” wide. The full cubic capacity for a 53-foot dry van trailer is 3,950 cubic feet.

What Are Air Ride Dry Van Trailers?

Equipped with a unique air ride suspension system, air ride dry van trailers use compressed air to inflate airbags, which act like springs and help to absorb shocks during transit. It's a simple concept, but it can significantly protect your cargo from damage.

Are Dry Van Trailers Insulated?

No. Dry van trailers are not outfitted with any kind of insulation. If you need insulation to ship your freight, you'll want to look at reefer trailers.
