Choosing the Right Amount of High Bay LED Lights

The amount of high bay light fixtures needed is determined by the size of the space, ceiling height, and building usage. For ceiling heights of between 12-20 feet, high bay lights in the 125-175 watt (12000-21000 lumens) range are most commonly used.

As the ceiling height increases, the wattage and the lumen output should also increase:

  • 8-10 feet = 80-100 watts (10,000-12,000 lumens)
  • 12-20 feet = 125-175 watt (12,000-21,000 lumens)
  • 18-25 feet = 200-300 watt (22,000-35,000 lumens)
  • 20-30+ feet = 300-400 watt (35,000-47,000+ lumens)

Recommended spacing for LED high bay lights are as follows:

  • 10-foot ceiling — 12-15 feet apart
  • 20-foot ceiling — 15-19 feet apart
  • 30+ foot ceiling — 19-35 feet apart

Types of LED High Bay Lights

Choosing the right high bay LED light for your space is about energy efficiency, durability, and smart control. From sleek designs to powerful lumens, here's a closer look at the various types of high bay LED lights and how they can enhance your space.

Linear High Bay LED Lights

These are the most recognized types of high bay lighting. Linear high bay LED lights illuminate large indoor spaces with high ceilings, typically 20 to 45 feet. Their rectangular shape allows for broad light distribution, focusing brightness downward and outward to cover expansive areas. Unlike round or bell-shaped fixtures, linear high bays offer more coverage along aisles or long spaces. They can be installed directly onto ceilings or suspended using chains or cables, providing versatility for various layouts. These fixtures ensure bright, efficient lighting for warehouses, retail stores, and industrial environments.

UFO High Bay LED Lights

With their compact, circular shape, UFO high bay LED lights are ideal for commercial and industrial settings, such as factories, workshops, and assembly lines. They provide broad, powerful illumination, making them perfect for spaces with ceilings over 20 feet high. You'll also find them in gyms, recreational facilities, warehouses, and storage areas. With high-efficiency LED bulbs, these lights offer superior brightness and energy savings, making them a great choice for large event centers, conference halls, and other venues needing reliable overhead lighting.

Vapor-Tight High Bay LED Lights

For environments exposed to moisture, dust, or chemicals, vapor-tight LEDs are the perfect lighting solution. Their sealed design protects the lights from harsh conditions, making them ideal for food processing facilities, parking garages, and wet industrial areas.

Additional Features of LED High Bay Lights

High-Lumen High Bay Lights

These fixtures produce a high amount of light output (measured in lumens) and are designed for spaces requiring intense and widespread illumination. High-lumen high bay lights are ideal for areas with very high ceilings or where strong, consistent lighting is necessary for safety and productivity, such as warehouses, manufacturing plants, and sports facilities.

Motion Sensor High Bay Lights

Smart lighting meets energy savings with motion sensor high bay lights. Equipped with sensors that detect movement, these fixtures only activate when needed, making them ideal for spaces where continuous lighting isn't required. Adjustable settings allow for fine-tuning sensitivity and range to help cut energy costs and boost sustainability.

What are High Bay LED Lights?

High bay LED lights are used to illuminate spaces with high ceilings. That usually means ceilings ranging from 20 feet to approximately 45 feet. Low-bay lights, on the other hand, are used for ceilings 20 feet and under. Because a high-ceiling location has more space to fill, a high bay light by definition is a powerful light source that can brighten up a large area.

Where is LED High Bay Lighting Used?

What is the maximum lumen output available for high bay LED lights?

High bay LED lights can reach over 60,000 lumens, making them ideal for spaces with high ceilings or demanding lighting requirements.

How do high lumen high bay lights improve visibility and safety in large spaces?

They provide bright, even coverage, reducing shadows and dark spots to ensure clear visibility, which enhances safety and productivity

Are high-lumen lights energy-efficient despite their high brightness?

Yes. LEDs produce more lumens per watt than traditional lights, delivering bright illumination while consuming minimal energy.

How does the lumen output affect the number of fixtures needed in a space?

Higher lumen output means fewer fixtures are required to achieve the desired brightness, lowering installation and energy costs.
