1. Embrace Natural Elements

Integrate the natural beauty of the waterfront into your landscaping. Use native plants and materials like stone and wood to create a seamless transition between your property and the natural environment. You can also incorporate features like fountains, ponds, or small waterfalls to complement your waterfront property. These additions improve the aesthetic appeal and provide soothing sounds that promote relaxation.

2. Create Cozy Seating Areas

One of the best waterfront landscaping ideas is creating a warm, welcoming gathering place to destress. Simply arrange your lake house furniture to suit your entertainment style. Do you host outdoor dinner parties? Serve cocktails and hors d'oeuvres al fresco? Or simply embrace the company of your inner circle? However you enjoy your backyard waterfront, there's durable outdoor furniture to suit your needs:

  • Lakeside Lounges: Arrange comfortable seating areas that offer clear views of the water. Opt for lake house furniture that's both stylish and weather-resistant. Think Adirondack gliders, rockers, and chairs with soft cushions to create a cozy atmosphere.
  • Fire Pits: A fire pit is a lovely huddle spot for chilly evenings. Surround it with Adirondack bar chairs to create a gathering place for friends and family to enjoy the warmth and the view.

3. Design With Privacy in Mind

Use tall grasses, shrubs, and trees to create natural privacy screens, a move that adds greenery and provides a sense of seclusion without obstructing the view of the lake. Constructing a pergola or gazebo can offer a shaded retreat while providing an elegant touch to your outdoor space. These structures can be adorned with climbing plants or outdoor curtains for additional privacy and charm.

4. Incorporate Functional Zones

Designate a space for outdoor dining with a sturdy outdoor table and chairs. Consider placing this area near the kitchen for convenience or by the water for a dining experience with a view.

Enhance your entertaining capabilities with an outdoor kitchen. Equip it with essentials like a grill, sink, and mini-fridge. This setup allows you to prepare meals without missing out on the outdoor fun. Or create an outdoor bar area where you can dazzle guests with your bartending skills. Outdoor counter-height bar tables are ideally suited for this setup.

5. Lighting for Ambiance

Use soft, ambient lighting to set the stage for a relaxing atmosphere in the evening. String lights, lanterns, and pathway lights can add a magical touch to your lakefront space. Solar-powered lights are eco-friendly and perfect for illuminating walkways, decks, and other areas without the need for extensive wiring.

6. Maintain Open Views

Choose low-profile furniture that doesn't block the view of the lake. Benches, chaise lounges, and low coffee tables provide functionality while preserving the natural sightlines. For safety without compromising the view, consider installing glass railings on decks and patios. They provide a barrier while allowing uninterrupted views of the waterfront. This is essential if you have (or entertain) little ones.
