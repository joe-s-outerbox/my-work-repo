What Are Your Poly Porch Rockers Made From?

DuraWeather's porch rockers are crafted from commercial-grade high-density polyethylene (HDPE), which is 100% recyclable and sustainable. This material originates from hundreds of thousands of recycled plastic bottles. After being transformed into pellets, they're melted and combined with weather-resistant additives. Not only do our Adirondack rocking chairs, outdoor dining sets and accessories look amazing, they are good for the environment. Each year, approximately 210 million recycled plastic bottles are repurposed into our durable and eco-friendly poly porch rockers!

How Do I Care for My Porch Rocker?

Maintaining your DuraWeather poly porch rocker is simple, ensuring it looks great for over 20 years. Our poly rockers require minimal maintenance. Here's how to keep them clean:

  • Regular Cleaning: Mix dish soap and water in a bucket. Use a hand towel or a soft-bristled brush to remove water spots and dirt. The HDPE material doesn't stain or damage easily, maintaining its color and finish​​.
  • Dealing with Stains: In case of tough stains or build-ups, mix 1/3 bleach with 2/3 water. Apply the mixture, let it sit for a few minutes, then scrub gently with a soft-bristled brush​​. Rinse well and air dry.

These steps will keep your poly porch rocker looking new, requiring only occasional cleaning despite being left outdoors year-round​

Can I Power Wash My HDPE Adirondack Rocking Chair?

Yes, you can powerwash all of our poly porch rockers and other furniture if needed. Because of their HDPE construction, our poly rockers naturally resist dirt, grime and environmental elements, making power washing a rarely needed option. Make sure that the pressure is below 1500 PSI and keep the nozzle at least 12 inches away from your porch rocker​​.
