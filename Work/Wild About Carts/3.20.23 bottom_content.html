The primary difference between gas and electric golf carts is their power source. Gas golf carts typically run on gasoline or diesel fuel, while electric golf carts rely on battery power. Gas golf carts tend to be more powerful and faster than electric models, making them a good choice for more challenging terrain or for those who prefer a speedier ride. However, gas carts require regular maintenance, including oil changes and fuel filter replacements, in addition to emitting fumes and noise. Electric golf carts are known for their quiet operation and low environmental impact. They require less maintenance than gas carts and are less expensive to operate over time. However, electric carts have a limited range on a single charge, and the batteries may need to be replaced every few years.
