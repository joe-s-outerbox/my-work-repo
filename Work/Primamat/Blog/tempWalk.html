Why Use a Portable Sidewalk?

A portable sidewalk is a path set up for a short period of time to help people or vehicles move safely and easily over an unstable or uneven area. It's not meant to be permanent but serves an essential purpose when needed.

There are many situations where temporary walkways come in handy. For example, on construction sites, workers need a safe and stable path to move around without tripping over uneven ground or getting stuck in the mud. At outdoor events like festivals or weddings, guests need a clear path, especially if the ground is soft or muddy. In landscaping projects, temporary walkways can protect the grass and plants from being trampled while work is done.

Using a temporary walkway has several benefits:

  • Increased Safety: A temporary walkway makes the area safer by providing a stable surface to walk or drive on, reducing the risk of slips and falls.
  • Improved Accessibility: A portable sidewalk makes it easier for everyone, including people with mobility issues, to get where they need to go.
  • Better Ground Protection: A temporary pathway also protects the ground underneath from damage, which can be important for preserving lawns, gardens, or other delicate surfaces.

A PrimaMat temporary sidewalk provides all these benefits. Whether you need a temporary walkway for muddy or grassy terrain, our mats deliver the convenience and protection you need.

Planning Your Temporary Walkway

You probably have several temporary walkway ideas. Before you start building your portable sidewalk, a little planning is essential. This will help ensure your walkway is safe and effective and meets your needs.

1. Think About Site Condition

First, take a good look at the site where you want to place the walkway. Check the terrain and the environment. Is the ground flat, or are there slopes and bumps? Is the area likely to get muddy or wet? Knowing the conditions will help you better prepare.

2. Consider Temporary Pathway Options

Carefully consider the location for your temporary sidewalk. Place it where people will actually walk, not where you'd like them to.

3. Measure for Your Temporary Walkway

Next, measure the area where the walkway will go. Use a tape measure to determine the length and width of the path you need. Make sure to account for any turns in the path. It's a good idea to mark the edges with stakes and string to clearly outline where the walkway will be.

4. Choose the Right PrimaMat Product

Then select the ground protection mats that are right for the job. Some of your options include:

  • Double-sided 3'x8' ground protection mats for extra grip
  • Yellow 4'x8' ground protection mats for extra visibility
  • 40" X 10' track mats for extra length

Whichever you choose, we build our mats to resist cracks, insects, and extreme hot and cold weather. They're all made of 100% recycled polyethylene for an eco-friendly boost.

Installing Your Portable Sidewalk

Now that you've planned your portable sidewalk and selected your mats, it's time to install the walkway. Follow these steps to lay down your mats properly and ensure they stay in place.

  1. Lay the First Mat: Start at one end of your marked path. Place the first mat on the ground, ensuring it's aligned with your outline.
  2. Add More Mats As Needed: Continue placing mats along the path, making sure each one fits snugly against the previous one.
  3. Connect Multiple Sections: Use our steel connectors to secure the mats to each other.
  4. Ensure Stability and Safety: Once all the mats are in place, walk along the path to check for loose or uneven sections. Make adjustments as needed to make sure the entire walkway is stable.

Following these steps will help ensure the proper installation of your portable sidewalk, whether you're creating temporary walkways for construction sites or special events.

Temporary Walkway Care and Maintenance

Once you set up your temporary walkway, keeping it in good shape is important. Regular maintenance and safety checks will ensure it stays safe and functional.

  • Walk along the pathway and look for any signs of damage or shifted mats. Pay special attention to high-traffic areas where wear and tear might be more noticeable.
  • Ensure the walkway is still level and all the mats are securely in place. If you find any sections that have become uneven or loose, re-connect them immediately.
  • Use a hose to wash off dirt and debris. Regular cleaning keeps your portable sidewalk looking good and helps prevent slips and falls. A clean walkway is a safe walkway.
