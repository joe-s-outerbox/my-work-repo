Why Upgrade to LED Street Rod Lighting?

LEDs aren't the only option for your taillights, but switching to LED brake lights for your street or hot rod is a smart move for several reasons. Let's break down the advantages of LED street rod lighting:

  • Brighter Illumination: LED lights are much brighter than traditional incandescent bulbs. This means your car will be more visible to other drivers, especially at night or in poor weather conditions. Better visibility equals better safety.
  • Longer Lifespan: LEDs last a lot longer than incandescent bulbs. While a regular bulb might burn out after a few thousand hours, an LED can keep shining for tens of thousands of hours. That means fewer replacements and less hassle for you.
  • Energy Efficiency: LED tail lights use less power than traditional bulbs. This can be a big deal for your car's electrical system, reducing the load and potentially extending your battery's life.
  • Faster Response Time: LED tail lights light up instantly, whereas incandescent bulbs take a fraction of a second to reach full brightness. That might not sound like much, but in a high-speed situation, every millisecond counts. Faster lighting can give the driver behind you more time to react, which can help prevent accidents.
Whether you're a night owl who loves late cruises or someone who wants their classic car to shine (literally) in any setting, LED lights are a no-brainer upgrade that pays dividends in both the short and long term.

Installation Tips for Hot Rod Tail lights

Installing LED tail lights on your hot rod might seem like a big job, but you can do it yourself with the right steps. We provide installation instructions with all our hot rod tail lights, but here's a simple guide to help you out:

  1. Gather Your Tools: Before starting, ensure you have all the necessary tools. You'll typically need a screwdriver, wire strippers, electrical tape, and possibly a drill.
  2. Remove the Old Tail lights: To avoid electrical issues, start by disconnecting your car's battery. Then, unscrew and carefully remove the old tail lights.
  3. Connect the Wires: Match the wires from your new LED tail lights to the existing wires in your car. Usually, there will be a ground wire, a brake light wire, and a turn signal wire. Use electrical tape or wire connectors to secure the connections.
  4. Mount the New Tail lights: Once the wiring is done, position the new LED tail lights in place. Make sure they fit snugly and align properly with the mounting holes. Use screws to secure them.
  5. Test the Lights: Reconnect the car battery and test the new tail lights before wrapping up. Check the brake lights, turn signals, and reverse lights to ensure everything is working correctly.

Double-check your measurements and make small adjustments as needed — don't force the lights. Always disconnect the battery before starting and check that your wire connections are properly insulated and secure. Lastly, take your time to ensure the tail lights on your hot rod are perfectly aligned.
