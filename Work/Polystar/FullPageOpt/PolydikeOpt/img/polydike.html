Poly Dike MPE® Containment Wall Structure and Installation

With the wall struts positioned, the entire secondary spill containment system ties together with a heavy-duty cabling harness assembly. Pigtails ending in solid steel D-rings support all four sides of the harness assembly. These D-rings slide into custom-designed slots in the wall struts where they are anchored in place by the locking connector. Stretching the harness assembly taut creates a unified, super-strong structure. After setting up the Poly Dike MPE portable spill containment wall, a flexible liner is rolled out and draped over the wall struts. Securing the liner in place with the innovative liner locks finishes the setup of your containment walls without damaging the liner. The Poly Dike spill containment dike is commonly bolted and sealed to a concrete pad or bolted together and used with a flexible liner.

How Do You Determine the Chemical Resistance of a Containment Wall?

The containment walls themselves are polyethylene and have a wide range of chemical resistance. However, the walls are used with a liner to create the containment. So, the only direct contact with the chemical should be with the liner. Several types of liner are available — matching the liner to the specific chemical would be the best way to determine chemical resistance.

Do You Provide the Liners for Containment Dikes?

We do not manufacture the liners used with the Poly Dike MPE. In the event you would like Polystar to source the liner, we do have relationships with liner providers that allow us to source the liner for you.

Is the Poly Dike MPE® secured to the substrate?

The Poly Dike MPE® is not secured to the substrate. It is designed to sit on a level surface to create the proper containment needed.

Do you provide installation for the Poly Dike MPE?

We do not provide installation, however, it is a simple process to install the system and requires no tools or heavy lifting equipment.
