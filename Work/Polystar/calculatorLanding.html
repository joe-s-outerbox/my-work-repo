What is Secondary Containment and Why Does It Matter?

Secondary containment is a system designed to catch spills or leaks from primary containment units like tanks or storage containers. It's a crucial part of environmental safety and compliance, especially for industries dealing with hazardous materials.

Federal laws, such as SPCC, mandate that businesses must have appropriate secondary containment to prevent costly fines and mitigate environmental risks.
