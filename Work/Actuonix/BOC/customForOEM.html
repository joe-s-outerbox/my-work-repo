Getting the Right Custom Linear Actuator for Your Project

Before engineering your custom linear actuator, let's begin by finding the right type of actuator for the job. Choose from a broad selection of products, including:

  • Rod Actuators: Rod actuators, often called rod-style linear actuators, consist of a rod that extends and retracts in a linear motion. These actuators are typically powered by electric, hydraulic or pneumatic means. Their simplistic design makes them a common choice for various applications, from industrial machinery to automotive lifts, where pushing or pulling force is required.
  • Feedback Actuators: Feedback actuators are custom linear actuators equipped with a feedback system, usually in the form of sensors, that provide real-time data on the actuator's position, speed or force. This allows for more precise control, monitoring and adjustments in applications requiring a high level of accuracy and repeatability.
  • Track Actuators: Track actuators, sometimes called track linear actuators, consist of a moving "carriage" or "block" that rides along an internal track. Unlike rod actuators that extend a rod outside their body, the moving part of a track actuator remains contained within its housing. This design is beneficial in applications where space is limited or where the actuator needs to remain compact during operation.

Types of Modifications We Can Make to Create Your Custom Linear Actuator

Typically, we can make modifications to our stock actuators with a minimum order quantity (MOQ) of 500pcs for changes such as:

  • Attaching your choice of electrical connector
  • Changing the length of the connection wire
  • Modifying the position of the limit switches
  • Shortening the stroke of the actuator below our stock products
  • Supplying a custom rod end attachment fitting
  • Fitting a non-stock gear ratio motor to alter the speed/force characteristics
  • Fitting a motor with a different operating voltage
  • Substituting the lubricants used to better facilitate low-temperature operations

Minimum order quantities (MOQ) may vary depending on the level of customization that you require.
