How Do I Keep My Door Hinges Looking Like New?
Use a soft, damp cloth to wipe the surface. For metal hinges, a mild soap solution can be used. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials, as these can damage the finish. Periodically, lube the door hinges with WD-40 or petroleum jelly to keep them from squeaking. Finally, tighten any loose screws.

Types of Residential Hinges for Cabinet Doors

There are three primary types of residential hinges, each with its unique uses and benefits. The type of door hinge you choose will depend on the type and weight of the door, your aesthetic preferences and your functional needs.

Butt Hinges

Butt hinges are the most common hinges for residential cabinet doors. Named due to their design where the two plates butt up against each other, butt hinges are durable and can carry a lot of weight. They're ideal for most types of interior and exterior doors. Their classic design blends well with any decor, and they can be painted over to match the door or trim color.

Pivot Hinges

Instead of being attached to the side of the door and the frame, pivot hinges are installed at the top and bottom of the door. This design makes them ideal for large, heavy doors, as the weight is distributed evenly over the floor and the top frame, reducing strain on the hinge. Pivot hinges provide smooth and quiet operation and offer a clean, modern aesthetic.

Concealed Hinges

Also known as European hinges or cup hinges, are concealed residential hinges popular for their seamless look. As the name suggests, these hinges are hidden inside the door when closed, offering a neat finish. The main benefit of concealed hinges is their aesthetic appeal. Furthermore, they offer adjustable settings for alignment, ensuring a perfect fit and smooth operation.

Choosing the Best Finish for Residential Cabinet Door Hinges

Hardware finishes for residential door hinges protect the underlying materials and significantly contribute to the aesthetic appeal of your hardware accessories. Let's take a closer look at some common finishes and their unique characteristics:

  • Antique Brass: With its timeless look, warm golden hues and aged appearance, antique brass complements traditional or rustic decor, adding a touch of elegance and history.
  • Antique Pewter: Its rich, dark gray hue carries a slightly weathered or distressed look. Antique pewter pairs well with both modern and vintage styles, bringing an old-world charm to the interior.
  • Chrome: This bright, shiny finish has contemporary appeal. It's highly reflective, creating a sleek look. Chrome is also resistant to tarnish and corrosion.
  • Polished Brass: With a shiny, golden color, polished brass brings a touch of luxury and sophistication to residential cabinet hardware hinges. It's often associated with traditional and formal decors, although it can also add an unexpected pop of brightness in modern settings.
  • Satin Brass: This is a softer, muted golden tone compared to polished brass. It offers a warm, sophisticated finish without the high shine for optimal versatility.
  • Satin Nickel: With a soft, subtle sheen and smooth texture, satin nickel's silver-gray color gives it a contemporary feel to residential hinges for cabinets and pairs well with various color palettes.
  • Solid Brass: Because it's made entirely of brass, you get a rich, high-quality appearance with excellent corrosion resistance. The color ranges from bright, shiny gold to a deeper, muted gold, depending on whether it's polished or left in a more natural state.
  • Venetian Bronze: Venetian bronze is a rich, dark finish with warm copper undertones. It often includes subtle brush strokes that add depth and character that pairs well with rustic, traditional, or Mediterranean styles.
  • White/Black: Solid color finishes create a clean, fresh look. These residential door hinges create a modern, minimalist aesthetic that partners easily with various materials.
