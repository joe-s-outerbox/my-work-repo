Types of Low Bay Lighting

The different types of LED low bay lighting offer versatile solutions for various applications by enhancing visibility, productivity and energy efficiency in indoor spaces. Low bay light fixtures combine style and function to provide the best kind of lighting for your commercial or industrial building. The two types of LED low bay lights are:

  • Round (or UFO): Characterized by their round, disc-like shape, UFO low bay lights provide 360-degree lighting distribution. They're typically suspended from the ceiling and offer wide-angle illumination, making them suitable for large open areas such as warehouses, factories and gymnasiums.
  • Linear: These elongated fixtures are commonly found in commercial and industrial settings, such as supermarkets, storage facilities and parking garages. Linear low bay LED lights provide evenly distributed light, ensuring excellent visibility along the length of the space.

Do I Need High Bay or Low Bay Lighting?

The choice between high bay and low bay lighting depends on the height of the ceiling in your space:

  • High Bay Lighting: Best for ceilings 20 feet or higher, such as warehouses, factories, gymnasiums, or large retail stores. High bay lights provide powerful illumination with a wide, focused beam to ensure adequate light reaches the floor.
  • Low Bay Lighting: Ideal for ceilings 20 feet or lower, such as in garages, workshops, or smaller retail spaces. Low bay lights have a diffused beam spread, making them suitable for shorter distances.

Consider the ceiling height, the size of the area, and the required brightness when making your decision.

Are Low Bay LED Fixtures Easy to Install?

Low bay LED fixtures are generally easy to install. Available with various mounting options that allow for convenient installation on ceilings or suspension from chains or hooks. Some low bay light fixtures may also have adjustable brackets or arms to assist with positioning and directing the light as needed.

What's the Difference Between LED Low Bay Lights and High Bay Lights?

Following are the key distinctions between LED low bay lights and high bay lights:

  • Ceiling Height: LED low bay lights are designed for spaces with lower ceilings, typically up to 15 feet in height, while high bays are engineered for ceilings 15 feet or higher.
  • Lumen Output: High bay lighting generally has a higher lumen output than low bay lighting to compensate for the greater distance between the fixture and the working plane. The higher lumen output ensures that the required light levels are maintained even at greater heights.
  • Illumination Intensity: High bays produce high-intensity illumination to properly light up large spaces with high ceilings, ensuring sufficient visibility for tasks and activities. Low bays provide focused illumination for areas with lower ceilings, offering adequate lighting levels for specific workstations or retail displays.
