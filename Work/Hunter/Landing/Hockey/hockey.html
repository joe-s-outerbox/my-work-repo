When planning the installation of HVLS ceiling fans in ice hockey arenas, it's crucial to consider several key factors to ensure optimal performance and airflow. Here's a guide to help you get started:

  • Fan Size to Coverage Ratio: Each HVLS fan operates best within a range of five times its blade diameter. For instance, a 20-foot fan is most effective in an area of about 15,000 square feet.
  • Assessing Coverage Needs: Accurately determining your arena's coverage requirements is essential. Overestimating a fan's capacity can lead to underperformance in cooling or heating.
  • Multiple Fans for Larger Areas: In some cases, using two smaller fans may be more effective than one large fan, especially if your arena's space exceeds the optimal coverage area of a single fan.
  • Ceiling Height Considerations: Knowing the ceiling height of your ice hockey arena is crucial for two reasons:
  • Ideal Fan Height: For maximum performance, HVLS fans should be positioned 20-30 feet above the ground. This height lets the fan move cooled or heated air to the floor level effectively.
    • Ensuring the fan can push air to the floor for effective temperature control.
    • Determining whether a longer downrod is needed to compensate for high ceilings.
  • Accounting for Airflow Obstacles: It's important to consider any potential airflow obstacles, such as racking or other structures, in your layout planning. These can impact the effectiveness of the fan's air distribution.
