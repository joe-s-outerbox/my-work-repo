As the holiday season rolls around, there's no need to miss out on the festivities just because you've decided to slow down or skip the booze. Unexpected guests? Don't fret. Your secret weapons for hosting are here: holiday mocktails.

These non-alcoholic drinks are as fancy and flavorful as their spirited counterparts. So good news for the designated drivers, expectant mothers, and everyone else joining the fun.

Let's raise a glass to the season of cheer! 

RUM HORCHATA LATTE

Horchata is a classic Latin American winter drink made from milk, rice, vanilla, and cinnamon. Surprisingly, rice adds a rich creaminess that results in a full-bodied, sweet treat. The kids can drink it as-is and adults tend to add a splash of Ritual Rum Alternative. It's not just a beverage, it's a festive celebration in a glass, and this non-alcoholic version means you can share the joy all night long.

1 1/2  oz. Ritual Rum Alternative
1 1/2 oz. horchata
1 oz. espresso
Garnish: freshly grated cinnamon

Directions: Combine the ingredients in a glass over ice and stir gently. Top with fresh grated cinnamon.

CANDY CANE SWIZZLE

Treat yourself to this delicious holiday cocktail made with non-alcoholic spirits. A holiday take on the well-known Army & Navy cocktail, this Candy Cane Swizzle incorporates candy cane syrup - a minty finish to the sweet almond flavors from orgeat. 

1 1/2 oz. Ritual Gin Alternative
1 oz. lemon juice
1/4 oz. orgeat
1/2 oz. candy cane syrup
float of Peychaud’s bitters (or your favorite non-alc bitters)
Garnish: candy cane

Directions: Combine the ingredients in a Collins glass and fill with crushed ice until the glass is two-thirds full. Swizzle together using a bar spoon. Top with more ice and add garnish.

TIZ' THE SEASON TODDY

Get in the holiday (non-alcoholic) spirits with this delicious warm cocktail. Kick your winter blues to the curb with a warming Hot Toddy mocktail featuring spiced cranberry syrup. This drink marries all the festive flavors of the season, served hot with a generous splash of your favorite non-alcoholic spirit. 

2 oz. Ritual Whiskey Alternative

1 oz. lemon juice
3/4 oz. spiced cranberry honey syrup
Hot water
Garnish: whole star anise & cinnamon

THE COZY BLANKET

Nothing is more fitting of a winter cocktail than one that just tastes cozy. Pomegranate, a favorite Mediterranean fall fruit, blends deliciously with tequila and lime, and surprisingly well with cinnamon simple syrup. This zero proof cocktail will be a pleasant surprise for partygoers, whether they're mocktail drinkers, cocktail drinkers, or both!

1 1/2 oz. Ritual Tequila Alternative
1/2 oz. cinnamon syrup
3/4 oz. lime juice
1 tbsp. pomegranate seeds
2 dashes Angostura bitters (or your favorite non-alc bitters)
Garnish: lime wheel

Directions: Muddle the pomegranate seeds with the syrup, bitters, and juice. Add all remaining ingredients plus ice and shake. Double strain into a glass over ice and garnish with a lime wheel.

CHRISTMAS BUCK

Looking for a holiday mocktail on the sour side? This recipe is a merry mix of lemon juice, sweet cranberry juice, a dash of bitters, ginger beer, and Ritual Whiskey Alternative. This drink is a wonderful holiday play on the classic Kentucky Buck.

5 oz. Ritual Whiskey Alternative
3/4 oz. lemon juice
1/2 oz. cranberry juice
3-5 dashes of bitters
2 oz. ginger beer

Directions: Add all ingredients except ginger beer to a cocktail shaker with ice and shake. Strain into a glass with ice and top with ginger beer. Garnish with a lemon wheel and fresh cranberries.

RUM HOT CHOCOLATE

Who doesn't love a warm, creamy hot chocolate to drive away the winter chill? Our Rum Hot Chocolate recipe is delightfully rich, has a cheeky hint of spiced rum flavor, and the best part? Have a few of these, and you’ll still wake up refreshed tomorrow.

Serves two

4 oz. Ritual Rum Alternative
1 1/2 C. milk
1/4 C. chocolate chips
1 tbsp. caramel
1/2 tsp. sea salt

Directions: Add milk, chocolate, caramel, and salt to a saucepan over medium heat. Stir until chocolate is melted and all ingredients are well blended. Add hot chocolate to two mugs. Add 2 oz of rum alternative and stir. Top with whipped cream and more caramel.

POMEGRANATE GINGER PALOMA

Palomas conjure up thoughts of Cozumel and Taco Tuesdays, but this spin makes for a tasty treat with more of a 'Feliz Navidad' attitude. Spicy ginger compliments the sweet citrus notes of pomegranate and grapefruit wonderfully in this invigorating blend. 

GINGER SYRUP:
1/2 C. honey
1 inch fresh ginger chopped
Mint leaves

Directions: Boil water, honey, and ginger over heat. Simmer for 1-2 minutes, remove from heat, and add mint. Wait 10 minutes. Strain out the ginger and mint.

PALOMA:
1 1/2 oz. Ritual Tequila Alternative
1 1/2 oz. pomegranate juice
1 1/2 oz. grapefruit juice
1/2 oz. lime juice
2-4 tablespoons ginger syrup
Ginger beer or sparkling water to top
Crushed ice

Directions: Fill your glass halfway with ice. Add the pomegranate juice, grapefruit juice, lime juice, syrup, and Ritual Tequila Alternative. Stir to combine and then top with ginger beer. Garnish with mint. enjoy!

Cranberry juice and...drum roll please...cinnamon-sweet potato syrup! This easy mocktail is deliciously crisp and won't leave you feeling foggy-headed in the morning.

1 1/2 oz. Ritual Rum Alternative
1/2 oz. cinnamon-sweet potato syrup

MEXICAN HOT CHOCOLATE

Nothing screams "holiday cheer" more than a steaming mug of hot chocolate. Packed with real dark chocolate and satisfying dulce de leche, this drink will have even the biggest Scrooges humming Jingle Bells.

3/4 C. Ritual Whiskey Alternative
4 C. whole milk
2 C. half-and-half
1/2 C. dulce de leche
10 oz. 70% dark chocolate
2 tsp. pure vanilla extract
1/4 tsp. Kosher salt
2 Cinnamon sticks

Directions: Whisk together milk, half-and-half, dulce de leche, and cinnamon sticks in a large saucepan. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat; cover and let stand for 10 minutes. Return mixture to a simmer over medium heat. Gradually whisk in finely chopped 70 percent dark chocolate, whisking until melted and smooth. Remove from heat and stir in pure vanilla extract, kosher salt, and Ritual Whiskey Alternative. Discard cinnamon sticks. Serve warm.

BOURBON CHAMPAGNE MOCKTAIL

Say cheers this winter with a Bourbon Champagne Mocktail, our spin on Kentucky's countryfied mimosa. Feel free to swap in whiskey instead of our Zero Proof version or champagne instead of cider for a low-abv version. Legend has it that Santa himself prefers this to milk and cookies...

2 oz. Whiskey Alternative
1/2 oz. orange juice (low abv: triple sec)
4 dashes bitters
4 oz. sparkling cider (low abv: champagne)
Garnish: orange twist 

Directions: In a cocktail shaker filled with ice, combine Whiskey Alternative and orange juice. Shake gently. Strain into a champagne flute. Top with bitters, then sparkling cider. Garnish with an orange twist.

HOLIDAY EGGNOG

This eggnog recipe has the works - cloves, cinnamon, ginger, vanilla, and more. Something about homemade eggnog tastes way more satisfying, especially when it's spruced up with our Whiskey Alternative. Plus, it's got all the bark and the bite of the real thing.

3/4 C. Ritual Whiskey Alternative
1 C. heavy cream
1 (1-inch) piece ginger, sliced
2 tsp. pure vanilla extract
8 whole cloves, crushed
6 allspice berries, crushed
2 cinnamon sticks, broken
1/4 tsp. freshly grated nutmeg, plus more for serving
4 C. whole milk, divided
8 large eggs, separated
3/4 C. granulated sugar
Pinch kosher salt

Directions: Combine heavy cream, ginger, vanilla, cloves, allspice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and 2 cups milk in a medium saucepan. Cook over medium heat until tiny bubbles begin to appear around the edge of the pot (do not let boil), 2 to 3 minutes. Remove from heat and let steep 30 minutes. 

Meanwhile, beat egg yolks with an electric mixer on medium speed until combined. Gradually beat in sugar and salt. Increase mixer speed to high and beat until thick and very pale in color, 2 to 3 minutes.

Strain spiced cream mixture, discarding spices; return to pot. Slowly whisk 1 cup warm cream mixture into yolk-sugar mixture on low speed; return to pot with cream mixture. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly with a rubber spatula, until mixture reaches 170°F and coats the spatula, 12 to 15 minutes.

Place the pot over a large bowl filled with ice water and let cool, stirring occasionally, until chilled, 20 to 30 minutes. Stir in Ritual Whiskey Alternative.

Just before serving, beat egg whites with an electric mixer on medium-high speed until soft peaks form, 1 to 2 minutes; fold into eggnog. Serve topped with nutmeg.

Bartender Bonus

Whether you're mixing cocktails in a bustling bar or for an intimate get-together, homemade ingredients make the experience far more memorable. We've created a few special blends you can make at home that encompass the diverse flavors of winter. You'll find these syrups in the Christmas mocktails above, but we'd love for you to get creative and mix up some of your own recipes!

CANDY CANE SIMPLE SYRUP

1 C. of Water

12 standard-size candy canes, 5 1/2 inches

Unwrap candy canes and place in a pot with 1 cup of water. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly. Remove from heat and continue stirring until candy is dissolved completely. Let cool and store refrigerated for up to 1 week. Best if chilled overnight but can be used immediately.

CINNAMON SIMPLE SYRUP

1 C. granulated sugar

1 C. water

4 cinnamon sticks, broken into large pieces

Bring all ingredients to a boil over medium heat. Reduce heat to medium-low and let simmer for 8-10 minutes until sugar has dissolved, stirring frequently. Remove from heat and let cool. Discard cinnamon sticks, strain into a clean glass jar, and cover. Store refrigerated for up to 2 weeks.

CRANBERRY HONEY SIMPLE SYRUP

1/2 cup clover honey 

1/2 cup water

1/2 cup fresh cranberries

In a small saucepan over medium heat simmer the water and the honey until honey fully dissolves into the water.  Add the fresh cranberries and continue simmering for 8 minutes (until the cranberries split or pop). Remove from heat and allow to cool. Place in a container with an airtight lid. Seal.
