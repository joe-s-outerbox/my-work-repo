Common Mistakes to Avoid When Using a Detail Sander

Detail sanders are straightforward tools, but there are pitfalls you might want to avoid to ensure you get the best out of your machine:

Over-Sanding

Detail sanders are designed for precision work, not for stripping large surfaces. Pressing too hard or using it for an extended period can lead to over-sanding, ruining the finish.

Ignoring Safety Protocols

While these machines may seem less intimidating than larger power tools, they still require proper safety gear. Always wear protective eyewear and a dust mask.

Using the Wrong Sandpaper Grit

The sandpaper you choose can significantly affect the outcome. Using a grit that's too coarse could scratch the surface, while one that's too fine may require more time and effort than necessary.

Neglecting Corners and Edges

Corners and edges are where detail sanders shine. Failing to adequately sand these areas can compromise the finished look. For these areas, the best sander for corners would be an ideal choice.
