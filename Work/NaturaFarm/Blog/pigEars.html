If given freely to them as a part of their regular diet, they risk becoming obese. A high-fat diet can lead to pancreatitis in many dogs, leading to extreme abdominal pain, diarrhea, and vomiting.

Frequently Asked Questions about Pig Ear Dog Treats

What are pig ears made of?

Natural Farm’s pig ear treats use the pinna or the pig's ear flap, primarily made up of skin and cartilage.

Do vets recommend pig ears for dogs?

Some vets may recommend pig ears as a treat for dogs in moderation, while others may advise against it due to the potential risks associated with them, such as bacterial contamination, choking, and digestive issues. It's best to consult with your veterinarian to determine if pig ears are a suitable treat for your dog.

Are pig ears highly digestible for dogs?

Pig ears are typically high in fat and can be difficult for some dogs to break down and absorb properly. Our pig ear treats are made with only one ingredient and are fully digestible. However, it's important to monitor their digestion and bowel movements any time you introduce a new treat to your pet.

How often can I give my dog pig ears?

The frequency of pig ear treats will depend on your dog's individual dietary needs and health status. In general, it's best to offer pig ears as an occasional treat, rather than a daily snack. Your veterinarian can advise you on how often you can give your dog pig ears.

How long should a dog chew on a pig ear?

There is no set time for how long a dog should chew on a pig ear. It will depend on the size and strength of your dog's jaw and their individual chewing style. However, we recommend giving your dog our pig ear treats for 3-5 minutes to start, and increasing time over a 5-day period. Make sure to provide plenty of water and never use pig ears as a replacement meal.

Can pig ears cause diarrhea?

Yes, pig ears can cause diarrhea in dogs, particularly if they are given in excess or if the dog has a sensitive stomach. If your dog experiences diarrhea after eating pig ears, it's important to stop giving them as a treat and consult with your veterinarian.
